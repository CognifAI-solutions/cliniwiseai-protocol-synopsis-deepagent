# 18. DATA HANDLING AND RECORD KEEPING

## 18.1. SOURCE DOCUMENTS

Source data are all information and original records of clinical findings, observations, or other activities in a clinical study necessary for the reconstruction and evaluation of the trial. Source data are contained in source documents.

Examples of these original documents and data records include, but are not limited to:

- Hospital records
- Clinical and office charts
- Laboratory notes
- Memoranda
- Patient memory aids (diaries) or evaluation checklists
- Pharmacy dispensing records
- Recorded data from automated instruments
- Copies or transcriptions certified after verification as being accurate and complete
- Microfiches
- Photographic negatives
- Microfilm or magnetic media
- X-rays
- Patient files and records kept at the pharmacy, laboratories, and medico-technical departments involved in the clinical study

Each participating site will maintain appropriate medical and research records for this trial in compliance with ICH E6(R2) guidelines and Indian GCP guidelines, along with the regulatory and institutional requirements for the protection of confidentiality of patients.

Sponsor and IRB/IEC will have access to medical and research records. Each site will permit Sponsor/Sponsor representatives, study Monitors, IEC/IRB, and regulatory agencies to examine (and, when required by applicable law, to copy) clinical records for the purposes of:

- Quality assurance reviews
- Audits
- Evaluation of study safety and progress

All the original records and computer-generated chromatograms during the bioanalytical phase will be treated as raw data/source documents.

Direct access to the source data/documents shall be permitted for study-related audits and regulatory inspections.

---

## 18.1. DATA COLLECTION

An electronic report/record form (eCRF) is required to be completed for each individual patient.

Electronic case report forms will be provided for transcription of all research data from source data to an electronic database.

Each responsible person at the site, <CRO name>, the Data Management team, or Sponsor will have user access with a unique username and password, with permissions providing each person the access required for their role.

Personnel will have permissions based on their individual study responsibilities, including:

- Data entry
- Data review
- Query resolution

The Principal Investigator is responsible for ensuring that the data entered into the eCRF is:

- Complete
- Accurate
- Updated in a timely manner

To facilitate accurate data entry in the eCRFs, eCRF Completion Guidelines (eCCGs) will be provided.

---

## 18.2. DATABASE MANAGEMENT

<CRO name> personnel will review the data entered by investigational staff for completeness and accuracy.

Electronic data queries stating the nature of the problem and requesting clarification will be created for discrepancies and missing values and sent to the investigational site via the EDC system.

Designated Investigator site staff are required to:

- Respond promptly to queries
- Make any necessary changes to the data

Medical history/current medical conditions, medications (including term and reason for taking), and adverse events will be coded using an appropriate medical coding dictionary.

The coding details and dictionaries used will be included in the Data Management Plan (DMP).

Central laboratory data will be processed centrally, and the results will be sent electronically to <CRO name> or the EDC system.

The database will be declared locked once data has been verified for completeness and accuracy.

Authorization is required prior to making any database changes to locked data through a joint written agreement between the Sponsor and <CRO name>.

For EDC studies, after database lock, the investigator will receive:

- CD-ROM copies, or
- Electronic copies, or
- Paper copies

of the patient data for archiving at the investigational site.

A separate Data Management Plan will be prepared describing the data management process.

---

## 18.3. RECORD RETENTIONS

Documents (Site Master File, source data, etc.) will be retained at the Investigator’s site as per applicable regulatory requirements, and the respective site Investigator will be responsible for the same.

<CRO name> will retain all study-related documents for a period of 25 years after completion of the study.

These documents may be retained for a longer period if required under an agreement with the Sponsor.

In such cases, it is the responsibility of the Sponsor to inform the Investigator/<CRO name> when these documents are no longer required to be retained.

The following records will be archived at their respective locations:

| Record Type | Archiving Location |
|---|---|
| Bioanalytical data | Bioanalytical site |
| Pharmacokinetic data | <CRO name> Global Research |
| Statistical data | <CRO name> Global Research |
