# 13. PATIENT SAFETY

## 13.1. ASSESSING, RECORDING AND ANALYZING SAFETY PARAMETERS

### 13.1.1. Adverse Events

As per Indian GCP guidelines, adverse event (AE) is *“Any untoward medical occurrence (including a symptom / disease or an abnormal laboratory finding) during treatment with a pharmaceutical product in a patient or a human volunteer that does not necessarily have a relationship with the treatment being given”.*

As per ICH-GCP E6 (R2) guidelines, adverse event (AE) is any untoward medical occurrence in a patient or clinical investigation patient administered a pharmaceutical product regardless of its causal relationship to the study treatment. An AE can therefore be any unfavorable and unintended sign (including an abnormal laboratory finding), symptom, or disease temporally associated with the use of medicinal (investigational) product.

All AEs must be graded for severity and relationship to IP. All AEs will be followed to adequate resolution.

All AEs including local and systemic reactions not meeting the criteria for “serious adverse events” should be captured on the pre-defined eCRF AE pages. Information to be collected includes event description, time of onset, clinician’s assessment of severity, relationship to investigational product and time of resolution/stabilization of the event. All AEs occurring while in the study must be documented appropriately regardless of relationship.

#### Severity of Event

Adverse events will be assessed and graded according to the Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events (CTCAE) version 5.0. Grade 1–5 will be used to characterize the severity of the Adverse Event.

If CTCAE grading does not exist for an adverse event, the severity of mild, moderate, severe, and life-threatening, death related to the AE corresponding respectively to Grades 1–5, will be used. Information about any deaths (related to an Adverse Event or not) will also be collected through a Death form.

For evaluating severity, following classification will be used to quantify intensity:

#### Table: Severity Classification of Adverse Event

| Grade | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Mild:** asymptomatic or mild symptoms; clinical or diagnostic observations only; intervention not indicated |
| 2 | **Moderate:** minimal, local or noninvasive intervention indicated; limiting age-appropriate instrumental Activities of Daily Living (refer to preparing meals, shopping for groceries or clothes, using the telephone, managing money, etc.) |
| 3 | **Severe or medically significant but not immediately life threatening:** hospitalization or prolongation of hospitalization indicated; disabling; limiting self-care Activities of Daily Living (refer to bathing, dressing and undressing, feeding self, using the toilet, taking medications, and not bedridden) |
| 4 | **Life threatening consequences:** urgent intervention indicated |
| 5 | **Death:** Any untoward medical occurrence that results in death |

Changes in the severity of an AE should be documented to allow an assessment of the duration of the event at each level of intensity to be performed. Adverse events characterized as intermittent require documentation of onset and duration of each episode.

#### Relationship to Investigational Products

The clinician’s assessment of an AE’s relationship to study medication is part of the documentation process, but it is not a factor in determining what is or is not reported in the study. If there is any doubt as to whether a clinical observation is an AE, the event should be reported.

All AEs must have their relationship to investigational product assessed using below mentioned terms. In a clinical trial, the study product must always be suspect. To help assess, the following classification will be used.

#### Table: Relationship Determination of Adverse Event with Investigational Product

| No. | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Certain**<br><br>• Event or laboratory test abnormality, with plausible time relationship to drug intake<br>• Cannot be explained by disease or other drugs<br>• Response to withdrawal plausible (pharmacologically, pathologically)<br>• Event definitive pharmacologically or phenomenologically (i.e. an objective and specific medical disorder or a recognised pharmacological phenomenon)<br>• Rechallenge satisfactory, if necessary |
| 2 | **Probable / Likely**<br><br>• Event or laboratory test abnormality, with reasonable time relationship to drug intake<br>• Unlikely to be attributed to disease or other drugs<br>• Response to withdrawal clinically reasonable<br>• Rechallenge not required |
| 3 | **Possible**<br><br>• Event or laboratory test abnormality, with reasonable time relationship to drug intake<br>• Could also be explained by disease or other drugs<br>• Information on drug withdrawal may be lacking or unclear |
| 4 | **Unlikely**<br><br>• Event or laboratory test abnormality, with a time to drug intake that makes a relationship improbable (but not impossible)<br>• Disease or other drugs provide plausible explanations |
| 5 | **Conditional / Unclassified**<br><br>• Event or laboratory test abnormality<br>• More data for proper assessment needed, or<br>• Additional data under examination |
| 6 | **Unassessable / Unclassifiable**<br><br>• Report suggesting an adverse reaction<br>• Cannot be judged because information is insufficient or contradictory<br>• Data cannot be supplemented or verified |
| 7 | **Not related**<br><br>• Any adverse event for which there is evidence that an extraneous cause(s) is present or for which no timely relationship exists to the administration of the study drug(s)/device(s)/intervention(s)<br>• The adverse event does not follow any previously documented pattern<br>• This category applies to those adverse events that are clearly not related to the study medication(s) |

If AE is ongoing at any visit, please follow-up the patient till the event gets resolved or condition gets stabilized.

### 13.1.2. Serious Adverse Event or Serious Adverse Drug Reaction

As per ICH-GCP E6 (R2), 21CFR312.32, ICMR guideline and Indian GCP guidelines, serious adverse event (SAE) or serious adverse drug reaction is any medical occurrence that at any dose:

| No. | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 | Results in death |
| 2 | Is life threatening |
| 3 | Requires inpatient hospitalization or prolongation of existing hospitalization |
| 4 | Results in persistent or significant disability/incapacity |
| 5 | Is a congenital anomaly or birth defect |
| 6 | Requirement of intervention to prevent permanent impairment or damage |

Any other important medical event that may not result in death, or is life threatening, or require hospitalization, may be considered a SAE when, based upon appropriate medical judgment, the event may jeopardize the patient and may require medical or surgical intervention to prevent one of the outcomes listed above.

Examples of such medical events include allergic broncho-spasm requiring intensive treatment in an emergency room or at home, blood dyscrasias or convulsions that do not result in inpatient hospitalization, or the development of drug dependency or drug abuse.

All SAEs will be recorded on the appropriate SAE form.

### 13.1.3. Abnormal Laboratory Values or Abnormal Clinical Findings

Laboratory abnormalities that are considered clinically significant by the investigator constitute an adverse event and should be recorded on the Adverse Events eCRF. Sample collection date and time shall be considered as AE onset date and time. Adverse event start time/date should be recorded from the date of sample collection.

Abnormal laboratory values or abnormal clinical findings at the time of screening will be considered as medical history and not AE.

Whenever possible, a diagnosis, rather than a symptom should be provided (e.g. anemia instead of low hemoglobin).

Laboratory abnormalities that meet the criteria for Adverse Events should be followed until they have returned to normal or an adequate explanation of the abnormality is found.

When an abnormal laboratory or test result corresponds to a sign/symptom of an already reported adverse event, it is not necessary to separately record the lab/test result as an additional event.

The investigator will evaluate the clinical significance of each laboratory value outside of the reference range.

- Laboratory parameter within the reference range will be considered as **“Normal”**
- Any laboratory parameter which is out of the reference range will be marked as:
  - **Abnormal clinically non-significant**, or
  - **Abnormal clinically significant**

This decision shall be based upon the nature and degree of the observed abnormality.

## 13.2. REPORTING OF SERIOUS ADVERSE EVENTS

Any AE considered serious by the investigator or sub-investigator or that meets the aforementioned criteria is subjected for expedited reporting.

Detailed reporting requirements will be discussed in a separate safety management plan for the study.

The Investigator will report SAEs to the sponsor, CBCC, CDSCO and ethics committee within 24 hours of its occurrence. In case the Investigator fails to report any SAE within the stipulated period, he/she shall have to furnish the reason for the delay to the satisfaction of the licensing authority along with the report of the SAE.

Investigator will submit the due analysis report of any SAE to CDSCO, chairperson of the ethics committee and head of the institution (study site) within 14 days of its occurrence. In case the Investigator fails to report any SAE within the stipulated period, he/she shall have to furnish the reason for the delay to the satisfaction of the licensing authority along with the report of the SAE.

The sponsor/CRO will submit a detailed report of any SAE to CDSCO, the IEC, and the head of the institution (study site) within 14 days of its occurrence.

The ethics committee will send its review report to CDSCO for any serious adverse event within 30 days of occurrence.

All serious and unexpected AEs will be followed until satisfactory resolution or until the investigator deems the event to be chronic or the patient to be stable.

The further course of action for all SAEs shall be observed as per current applicable rules and regulations.

## 13.3. PREGNANCY REPORTING AND FOLLOW-UP

All the female patients will be instructed to immediately inform the Investigator if they become pregnant after the first dose and till 8 weeks of the last dose administered.

Within 24 hours of becoming aware, the Principal Investigator or designee will complete a pregnancy report and forward to the Medical Monitor and Sponsor.

Follow-up information regarding pregnancy outcome and any postnatal sequelae will be collected and reported to Medical Monitor and Sponsor through a follow-up pregnancy report.

In case, the pregnancy does occur during the study, the further administration of the investigational product(s) shall be discontinued, and the participant shall be withdrawn from the study.

Moreover, such cases will be followed up till term or termination to ascertain the outcome of pregnancy, including:

- Spontaneous or voluntary termination
- Birth details
- Presence or absence of any birth defects
- Congenital abnormalities

Principal Investigator/designee shall follow-up during pregnancy at 3-month intervals till term or termination.

Post-delivery, infants will be followed-up by Investigator for a month or till evaluation done by the neonatologist/paediatrician.

Pregnancy alone is not regarded as an AE or SAE. Hospitalization for normal or elective caesarean section delivery will not be considered as SAE.

However, if there is an associated serious outcome like:

- Maternal serious complications
- Therapeutic abortion
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Stillbirth
- Neonatal death
- Congenital anomaly
- Birth defect

it will be considered as SAE.

All relevant details of pregnancy and its outcome till follow up will be recorded and reported by the investigator to the Medical Monitor and sponsor, and, if applicable, to IEC and the regulatory agency.

If the outcome of pregnancy qualifies for SAE, it will be reported as per SAE reporting obligation.

## 13.4. REPORTING OF OTHER ADVERSE EVENTS

All the AEs must be reported to the sponsor and IEC/IRB as per institutional or regulatory requirements.

## 13.5. MEDICAL MONITORING

It is the responsibility of the Investigator to oversee the safety of the study at site.

This safety monitoring will include:

- Careful assessment of AEs
- Appropriate reporting of AEs
- Construction and implementation of site data and safety-monitoring plan

For any patient safety or eligibility concerns or protocol related queries, the Investigator shall contact the medical monitor.

A separate medical monitoring plan may be prepared.
