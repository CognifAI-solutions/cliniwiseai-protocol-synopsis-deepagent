# 19. STUDY MONITORING, AUDITING AND INSPECTING

## 19.1. RESPONSIBILITIES OF INVESTIGATORS

The Investigator(s) is responsible to perform the clinical study in compliance and accordance with this clinical study protocol, ICH E6(R2) guidance and all other applicable guidelines and regulations. The Investigator is also responsible to provide reliable data and all information requested by the clinical study protocol (with the help of eCRF, or other appropriate mode of communication) in an accurate and legible manner according to the instructions provided and to ensure direct access to source documents by Sponsor/Sponsor representatives.

---

## 19.2. RESPONSIBILITIES OF SPONSORS AND STUDY MANAGEMENT PLAN

The Sponsor or Sponsor representative of this clinical study is responsible for taking all reasonable steps to ensure the proper conduct of the protocol as regards ethics, clinical trial protocol compliance, and integrity and validity of the data recorded on the eCRFs. Thus, the main role of the clinical monitoring team is to help the Investigator and the Sponsor in maintaining a high level of ethical, scientific, technical and regulatory quality in all aspects of the clinical study.

<CRO name> CRA will monitor sites. A Sponsor representative may accompany <CRO name> CRA. A detailed Project Management Plan, incorporating Clinical Operation and Monitoring Plan, Safety Management Plan, IP Management Plan, Medical Monitoring Plan and Data Management Plan will be generated outlining further management of the study-related activities of Sponsor and CRO.

---

## 19.3. AUDITING AND INSPECTING

The Investigator will permit study-related monitoring, audits and inspections by the IEC/IRB, the Sponsor, government regulatory bodies and quality assurance groups of all study-related documents (e.g. source documents, regulatory documents, data collection instruments, study data etc.). The Investigator agrees to allow the auditors or inspectors to have direct access to study records for review, being understood that these personnel are bound by professional secrecy, and as such will not disclose any personal identity or personal medical information. The confidentiality of the data verified and the protection of the patients should be respected during these inspections.

The Investigator will ensure the capacity for inspections of applicable study-related facilities (e.g. pharmacy, diagnostic laboratory, study documents etc.). Participation as PI in this study implies acceptance of potential inspection by government regulatory authorities and quality assurance offices. As soon as the Investigator is notified of a future inspection by the authorities, he/she will inform the CRO and CRO will inform the Sponsor and authorize the Sponsor to participate in these inspections. Any result and information arising from the inspections by the regulatory authorities will be immediately communicated by the Investigator to the CRO and Sponsor.

---

## 19.4. QUALITY CONTROL AND QUALITY ASSURANCE

Sponsor or the Sponsor’s designee will monitor the study and site activity while ongoing, to verify that the:

- Data are authentic, accurate and complete
- Safety and rights of the patients are being protected
- Study is conducted in accordance with the currently approved protocol and amendments and any other study agreements, GCP, and all applicable regulatory requirements

<CRO name> Quality Assurance (QA) and Sponsor shall perform retrospective audit of clinical raw data generated during the course of the study and the statistical data as per in-house SOPs to ensure compliance with this protocol, GCP and other applicable regulations.

The bioanalytical site will be responsible for quality assurance audit of bioanalytical data. At the bioanalytical facility, the study activities and study data of the bioanalytical phase will be liable for systematic and independent quality assurance audits, implemented and maintained as per written in-house SOPs of the bioanalytical site.

At the end of the study, the final study report will contain an “Audit Certificate” to assure that the evaluated study-related activities were conducted, and the data were generated, documented (recorded), analyzed, and accurately reported in compliance with study protocol, GCP, applicable principles of GLP, SOPs and applicable EMA regulatory requirements.
