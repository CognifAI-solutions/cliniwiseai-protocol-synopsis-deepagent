# Generating Ethical and Regulatory Standards

Use the below content. Use the section number and subsection number provided by the user. If CRO's name is provided replace <CRO name> with that name, else use 'CRO'

```
# 17. ETHICAL AND REGULATORY STANDARDS

## 17.1. ETHICAL PRINCIPLES, LAWS AND REGULATIONS

The clinical trial will be conducted as per the principles and requirements of the Declaration of Helsinki (*Ethical Principles for Medical Research Involving Human Patients*, revised by the 64th WMA General Assembly, Brazil, 2013), relevant EMA guidelines, and will be consistent with the ICH-GCP E6(R2) guidelines along with the local regulatory requirements of:

- GCP for Clinical Research in India (2004, CDSCO)
- New Drugs and Clinical Trials Rules, 2019 and further amendments by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India
- All applicable amendments under the Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945
- ICMR’s *National Ethical Guidelines for Biomedical and Health Research Involving Human Participants* (2017)

---

## 17.2. ETHICS COMMITTEE / INSTITUTIONAL REVIEW BOARD

Each participating institution/hospital must provide this protocol (and protocol amendment, if applicable) and associated documents for review and approval to the ethics committee (IEC) or institutional review board (IRB) registered with CDSCO for formal approval of the study conduct.

The decision of the IEC/IRB, signed by its chairman along with IEC composition details concerning the conduct of the study, will be made in writing to the investigator, and a copy of this decision will be provided to the sponsor before commencement of the study.

The Investigator should provide a list of IEC/IRB members and their affiliations to the sponsor.

If requested, a progress report during the trial and a summary of the study at the end of the clinical trial will be submitted to the IEC/IRB.

---

## 17.3. INFORMED CONSENT PROCESS

The Investigator or a person designated by the Investigator, under the Investigator’s responsibility, should completely inform the patients and/or their families about the study and provide sufficient information for making an informed decision regarding participation.

All patients should be informed to the fullest extent possible about the study in language and terms they are able to understand.

The consent form must be IRB/IEC approved, and the patient will be provided the consent form in the vernacular language of his/her choice.

Prior to participation in the study, the written informed consent form must be:

- Signed
- Name filled in
- Personally dated

by:

- The patient, or
- The patient’s legally acceptable representative / impartial witness (along with the patient’s thumb impression in cases where the patient is illiterate)

and by the person who conducted the informed consent discussion.

A copy of the signed and dated informed consent form will be provided to the patient.

Patients will be reimbursed for their travel/conveyance expenses.

---

## 17.4. PATIENT AND DATA CONFIDENTIALITY

Patient confidentiality, along with information disclosed/provided/produced by the Sponsor during the clinical study, including but not limited to:

- Clinical study protocol
- CRFs
- ICFs
- Study results

shall be strictly held in trust by:

- The Sponsor
- Sponsor’s authorized personnel
- Investigators and their staff members
- <CRO name> and its staff

No information concerning the study or the data will be released to any unauthorized third party without prior written approval from the sponsor.

The study Monitor or other authorized representatives of the Sponsor may inspect all documents and records maintained by the investigator, including but not limited to:

- Medical records (office, clinic, or hospital)
- Pharmacy records for the patients in the study

The clinical study site will permit access to such records.

However, submission of this clinical study protocol and other necessary documentation to the ethics committee (IEC/IRB) is expressly permitted, with the understanding that IRB/IEC members have the same obligation of confidentiality.

The European Union (EU) General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) will be followed for data protection and privacy.
```
